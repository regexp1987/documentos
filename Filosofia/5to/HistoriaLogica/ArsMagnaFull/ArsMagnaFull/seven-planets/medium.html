Book of the Natural Medium
or the art of generating sciences through the natural medium

Blessed Raymond Lull

Lord God! With your utmost blessing and virtue
Here begins the book of the natural medium.

     Because nearly all of us live in our world of opinions, and as opinion is a weakness of the intellect, and as the health of the intellect is in its act of understanding, since this act is the natural medium between the one who understands, and that which is understood, on this account, we now write this book about the permanent natural medium which exists between the subject and the predicate, without which the intellect cannot make any science at all.
     A predicate can belong to a subject in various ways through necessary predication, as when we say "Man is an animal". This kind of predication is necessary, because there is a given natural medium common to both the subject and the predicate. A natural common medium arises for instance when we say: "Peter is a man". And from these two propositions, there follows a natural conclusion, that is to say: "Therefore Peter is an animal".
     Jews and Christians and Muslims as well, all assert that when we say "the world was created", "the world" is the subject, and "was created" is the predicate. But there is no natural medium given between such a subject and predicate. And this is so for the reason that the world was not created in a natural way. And through this, faith and belief arise, and opinion, and the intellect does not produce any science through this kind of predication.
     We intend to write this book by means of the eighteen ultimately general principles, as we mix one principle with another, and track down the natural medium. Now the principles are the following:
1. Goodness, 2. Greatness, 3. Duration, 4. Power, 5. Wisdom, 6. Will, 7. Virtue, 8. Truth, 9. Glory, 10. Difference, 11. Concordance, 12. Contrariety, 13. Beginning, 14. Middle, 15. End, 16. Majority, 17. Equality, 18. Minority.
These principles are taken from the General Art, and its process is followed in this book.
     Now we will be tracking the natural medium through argumentation. And above all, having the Holy Spirit in mind, so that we might gain knowledge of It, as It is the natural medium proceeding naturally from God the Father and God the Son, for It is the Spirit of the Father and of the Son. And first, we will speak of Goodness.

     We have sought out the natural medium through eighteen natural principles. And through this kind of search, we have knowledge of the Holy Spirit through Its grace, as It is the natural medium, and thus we build science.
     And since the Holy Spirit is supreme, and since It cannot be without the Father and the Son, as has been shown in the search we have just done, the Holy Spirit is relatively the analogous example of all other natural media, such as the acts of seeing, hearing, smelling, tasting, touching, speaking, imagining, understanding, loving and remembering. And the same can be said for the acts of generating, elementing, vegetating; and likewise for all other acts.
     Having found such a supreme medium, we praise and adore It. And we truly say, that this is the medium through which man can generate sciences, of whatever kind they might be, by looking at the natural medium between the subject and the predicate. And so, this book can be called the art for generating sciences through the natural medium.

To the praise and honor of God, Raymond finished this book in the month of October in the city of Messina, in the year 1313 of the incarnation of our Lord Jesus Christ.
