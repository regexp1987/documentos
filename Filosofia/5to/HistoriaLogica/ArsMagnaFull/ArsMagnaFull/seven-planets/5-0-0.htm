Part five:  Questions about astronomy

In this part we intend to deal with questions about astronomy in three ways. First we put some questions that we solve right away.  Secondly  we put questions and indicate places in this book where solutions are found. Third, we solve questions according to the points we developed on our own in this treatise in accordance with necessary principles that provide answers we could not obtain by relying on the positions handed down by past astronomers.

Many questions can be raised regarding astronomy and as we want this treatise to be brief we do not want to put too many questions. The questions we put here with their solutions provide a doctrine for solving any other questions that can be asked about astronomy.
